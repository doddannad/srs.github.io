e-Stamp Paper

"e-Stamp Paper" or "Electronic Stamp Paper" refers to a digital or electronic version of a traditional paper document that has a special stamp or mark indicating payment of stamp duty. E-stamp papers are generated and issued online by authorized government agencies or approved service providers, and they are used as a valid alternative to physical stamp papers for various legal and business transactions.

E-stamp papers are created in a digital format and typically include a unique identification number, security features, and an electronic stamp or mark indicating the payment of stamp duty. They are generated using secure electronic systems and are typically tamper-proof and verifiable to ensure their authenticity and validity.

E-stamp papers are increasingly being used as a convenient and efficient way to create legally valid documents without the need for physical stamp papers. They are commonly used for agreements, contracts, deeds, affidavits, and other legal documents, and they are recognized and accepted by government authorities, courts, and other relevant stakeholders as valid evidence of payment of stamp duty and the legal validity of the document.

The use of e-stamp papers may be governed by specific laws, regulations, and guidelines in a particular jurisdiction, and it is important to comply with the applicable requirements when using e-stamp papers for legal or business purposes.
